Best Electric SUVs for Families in 2026: Top 7 Models to Buy

March 23, 2026 By Charity Ozor 18 min read
The 2026 family EV market has matured: longer range, faster charging, and genuine three-row options now available
The Family EV Dilemma: Solved

You need space for kids, cargo, and maybe a third row. You want safety, range, and a vehicle that won't break the bank. For years, that meant buying a gas SUV. In 2026, the equation has flipped. The electric SUV market now offers genuine three-row seating, 300+ miles of range, and charging networks that finally work for road trips. This guide walks you through the top 7 family electric SUVs available now—with real data on space, charging, performance, and which one fits your life.

1. 2026 Hyundai Ioniq 9: The 3-Row Family Champion

Hyundai Ioniq 9 $60,555 - $78,090 U.S. News Best Midsize EV SUV
MotorTrend Editor's Pick
311-335 mi range 7 seats 110.3 kWh 0-60 in 4.4 sec

If you need a genuine three-row SUV that can haul your family and all their stuff, the 2026 Hyundai Ioniq 9 is the new benchmark. MotorTrend tested it and concluded that it "counters basically every anti-EV argument" . With a massive 110.3-kWh battery, it delivers up to 335 miles of range—enough to beat its Kia sibling by as much as 81 miles .

Space where it matters: The third row comfortably fits 6-foot adults, a rare feat in the EV world . Cargo space behind the third row measures 21.9 cubic feet—just 2.6 cubic feet smaller than a Chevy Tahoe's, despite the Ioniq 9 being a foot shorter . The frunk, while partially occupied by charging equipment, frees up underfloor storage.

Family-friendly features: Universal Island 2.0 lets you slide the center console fore and aft, making it easy to pass snacks or hand items to the back without dislocating a shoulder . Available power captain's chairs in the first two rows include Relax Mode with footrests for deep recline . Ten airbags are standard, and it's targeting top safety ratings from IIHS and NHTSA .

Towing and capability: Need to bring a boat or travel trailer? AWD models tow up to 5,000 pounds; RWD models handle 3,500 pounds . The AWD Calligraphy model hits 60 mph in just 4.4 seconds in MotorTrend testing—quicker than the official estimate .

Charging and ownership: NACS port standard—direct access to Tesla Superchargers . On a 350-kW CCS charger, 10-80% takes 24 minutes; on 400-volt Tesla stations, about 40 minutes. Hyundai includes either a free home charger or $400 in public charging credits .

True adult third row 335-mile range 5,000 lb towing Pricey top trims Small rear hatch opening

2. 2026 Kia EV9: The Cool 3-Row SUV

Kia EV9 $65,000 (Wind AWD est.) IIHS Top Safety Pick+
MotorTrend 9/10
230-304 mi range 6-7 seats 76.1-99.8 kWh 230 kW charging

The Kia EV9 proves that three-row SUVs don't have to be boring. MotorTrend calls it "undeniably stylish," with a cabin that balances aesthetics with ease of use . For 2026, the EV9 gains a NACS charging port, granting direct access to Tesla's Supercharger network . A new Nightfall Edition adds blacked-out styling, and the high-performance GT variant delivers over 500 hp and a 4.3-second 0-60 time .

Performance options: Base powertrain: 76.1-kWh battery, 215 hp, 230 miles range. Long-range single-motor: 201 hp, 304 miles range. Dual-motor AWD: 379 hp, 443 lb-ft (516 with Boost), 4.4-second 0-60, 270-280 miles range . The new GT: 500+ hp, adaptive suspension, limited-slip diff, simulated transmission via paddle shifters .

Family credentials: The EV9 earned 2025 Top Safety Pick+ from IIHS and a 5-star NHTSA rating . Safety features include front automatic emergency braking, intersection collision avoidance, blind-spot monitoring, lane keep assist, adaptive cruise control, and road sign recognition .

Interior and tech: Dual 12.3-inch displays (infotainment + gauge cluster) plus a 5-inch climate touchscreen . Wireless Apple CarPlay/Android Auto, wireless charging, USB-C ports in all rows. Available 14-speaker Meridian sound system. Over-the-air updates keep software current .

Practicality: Cargo space is sufficient, though a bit more third-row legroom would be welcome . All U.S. EV9s are assembled at Kia's West Point, Georgia facility .

NACS port standard 500+ hp GT model Top Safety Pick+ Range trails Ioniq 9 Limited third-row legroom

smart #5 $45,000 (est.) What Car? Family EV of the Year
Euro NCAP Best in Class

The smart #5 has been quietly winning awards worldwide, and for good reason. At the 2026 What Car? Awards in London, it was crowned Family Electric SUV of the Year, praised for its "impressive range, fast charging, comfortable ride and high-quality interior" . It also earned Norway's 2026 Car of the Year, Euro NCAP's "Best in Class" as the Safest Large SUV of 2025, and two Red Dot Awards for Product Design .

Range and charging: With up to 590 km (367 miles) WLTP range, the #5 easily handles family road trips. Its advanced 800-volt platform enables charging from 10% to 80% in less than 18 minutes under optimal conditions—one of the fastest in its class .

Interior and space: The cabin features a 10.25-inch full HD digital instrument cluster and two 13-inch OLED screens (center and passenger) . With 630 liters of boot capacity, it's practical for strollers, sports gear, and Costco runs. Euro NCAP's "Best in Class" safety rating gives parents peace of mind .

Performance lineup: Available in multiple variants from entry-level Pro to high-performance BRABUS, with power up to 475 kW .

4. 2026 Ford Mustang Mach-E: The Sporty Family Hauler

9.2/10 U.S. News

The Mustang Mach-E proves that family SUVs can still be fun. U.S. News rates it 9.2/10 and ranks it #2 among electric SUVs, praising its "swift acceleration, excellent handling, and SUV practicality" . For 2026, the Mach-E adds a GT California Special package inspired by 1960s Mustang trims .

Powertrain lineup: Select RWD: 264 hp, 260 miles range. Premium AWD: 325 hp, 240-300 miles range. GT AWD: 480 hp, 280 miles range. Rally: 480 hp, 255 miles range . The GT with Performance Pack hits 60 mph in an estimated 3.3 seconds .

Family practicality: The cabin seats four comfortably (five in a pinch), though the sloping roofline limits rear headroom for taller teens . Cargo space is 29.7 cubic feet behind the second row, expanding to 59.7 with seats folded. The frunk adds 4.8 cubic feet of lockable storage—great for muddy boots or groceries .

Tech and safety: The 15.5-inch vertical touchscreen runs Ford's latest Sync system. Standard Ford Co-Pilot360 includes automatic emergency braking, blind-spot monitoring, and lane-keeping assist. Adaptive cruise control and 360-degree camera are available .

5. 2026 Chevrolet Equinox EV: The Value King

Chevrolet Equinox EV $34,995 - $44,095 U.S. News Best Value
9.0/10 U.S. News
307-319 mi range 5 seats 85 kWh battery 117 MPGe city

If budget is your primary concern, the Equinox EV delivers exceptional value. Every 2026 model now comes with a standard dual-level charging cord (120V/240V), and all trims offer at least 307 miles of range—starting at just $34,995 . U.S. News calls it "stylish, roomy and well-equipped," with easy-to-use systems that make daily life simpler .

Powertrain options: Front-drive single-motor: 220 hp, 243 lb-ft, 319 miles range. All-wheel-drive dual-motor: 300 hp, 355 lb-ft, 307 miles range, 0-60 in under 6 seconds .

Interior and space: At 190.6 inches long, the Equinox EV offers generous passenger room. Even 6-foot-4 drivers can sit comfortably behind themselves . Cargo space measures 26.4 cubic feet behind the rear seat and 57.2 cubic feet with the seat folded .

Charging and ownership: DC fast charging at up to 150 kW adds 77 miles in 10 minutes . In real-world testing, a 12-81% charge took 42 minutes. On a Level 2 home charger, it adds about 36 miles per hour .

6. 2026 Tesla Model Y Performance: The Practical Speedster

Tesla Model Y Performance $100,900 (NZ) / $53,990 (US base) Best Charging Network
5-Year Unlimited km Warranty
580 km WLTP (Performance) 5 seats 0-100 in 3.3 sec 854 L boot + 117 L frunk

The Model Y Performance answers the question: what if a family SUV could outrun a Porsche 911 GT3? With 0-100 km/h in 3.3 seconds and 741 Nm of torque, it's "the quickest family SUV you can buy," according to AutoTrader NZ . Yet it still offers 854 litres of boot space (plus a 117-litre frunk) .

Driving experience: The chassis gets unique springs, stabilizer bars, bushings, and adaptive damping . It's taut—passengers will feel sharper bumps—but the grip is immense, and the stability systems are well-judged . The spread between drive modes is narrower than expected; don't expect Comfort mode to transform it into a wafter .

Charging advantage: While maximum charging speed tops out around 250 kW (behind some 800V rivals), Tesla's Supercharger network remains "the gold standard" for reliability, locations, and seamless payment .

Full Self-Driving option: The supervised FSD system is now available via subscription, making it more palatable for families. It remains "a genuine usability advantage for those who do a lot of kilometres" .

7. 2026 Volkswagen ID.4: The Practical Used Value

Volkswagen ID.4 £10,000-£25,000 used (UK)
Best Used Value
224-350 mi range 5 seats 543 L boot

If you're shopping on a budget, the used ID.4 market offers incredible value. Motorpoint notes that "there isn't much that offers the same level of space for the money as a used VW ID.4 does" . Early examples with higher mileage can be found for under £10,000, and even well-equipped cars are affordable .

Range options: 52kWh battery: 224 miles WLTP (realistically less in winter). 77kWh battery: up to 350 miles WLTP, giving a usable 280 miles at 80% charge .

Family space: The 543-litre boot is among the best in class, and the tall roofline provides excellent headroom for all passengers . Rear legroom is generous even with front seats all the way back .

What to watch: The haptic touch controls remain frustrating—climate sliders are imprecise, and the two-window switches controlling four windows is needlessly complex . Basic trims with flat paint and hubcaps look "apologetic and drab" .

How to Choose Your Family EV

Need three rows regularly? Hyundai Ioniq 9 or Kia EV9 are your only real options. The Ioniq 9 has slightly more third-row space and range; the EV9 offers a high-performance GT variant .
Maximum range under $40k? Chevrolet Equinox EV delivers 319 miles starting at $34,995—unbeatable value .
Sporty driving with family utility? Ford Mustang Mach-E GT (3.3 sec 0-60) or Tesla Model Y Performance. The Tesla has better charging infrastructure; the Ford has a frunk and more engaging handling .
Budget-conscious used buy? Volkswagen ID.4 offers massive space for under £15,000, though you'll tolerate some touch-control frustrations .
Award-winning newcomer? smart #5 has swept European awards for its 367-mile range, 18-minute charging, and best-in-class safety .

Family EV Buying Tips for 2026

1. NACS is now standard. All 2026 models from Hyundai, Kia, Ford, and GM come with NACS ports, giving you direct Supercharger access . This is a game-changer for road trips.

2. Range matters, but charging speed matters more. A 300-mile EV with 150 kW charging will be slower on road trips than a 250-mile EV with 350 kW charging. Look for 800V architecture (Ioniq 9, smart #5) .

3. Real-world range in winter. Expect 20-30% range loss in cold weather. If you live in a cold climate, factor that into your range needs.

4. Towing capacity varies widely. The Ioniq 9 tows 5,000 lbs; the Equinox EV only 1,500 lbs . Know what you need before you buy.

5. Third-row space is not created equal. The Ioniq 9 and EV9 are the only EVs here that can genuinely fit adults in the back. Test-fit your teens before committing .

The Bottom Line

The 2026 family electric SUV market offers genuine choice for the first time. For three-row families, the Hyundai Ioniq 9 is the new king—more range, more space, and more thoughtful family features than anything else . If you can live with two rows, the Ford Mustang Mach-E and Chevrolet Equinox EV deliver sportiness and value respectively . And if you're on a tight budget, the used Volkswagen ID.4 market is a goldmine .

Test-drive the Ioniq 9 and EV9 back-to-back if you need three rows. For everyone else, the smart #5's combination of range, charging speed, and safety awards makes it a compelling wildcard . Whichever you choose, 2026 is the year family EVs finally became practical, affordable, and genuinely desirable.
